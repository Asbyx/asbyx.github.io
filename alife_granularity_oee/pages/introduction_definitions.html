Introduction & Key Definitions

The Wonder of Evolution

Evolution: a fascinating mechanism transforming randomness into information and function.

  • Creativity
  • Adaptation
  • Directed or Unbounded

Core Definition: Evolution

A never-ending cycle (mostly):

  • Birth with variation
  • Reproduction
  • Death

Standard definitions may not always perfectly fit all artificial scenarios.

Key Terms in This Context

  • Simulation (Alife Model): Software simulations aiming for "life in simulo," not just reproducing natural behaviors.
  • Component: An entity in the simulation (genetic sequence, blob, program, particle, etc.).
  • Fitness: Reproductive success score. A key focus. Note: components themselves might not have direct fitness scores (e.g., particles in particle life).

Open-Ended Evolution (OEE)

OEE is achieved when an evolutive system presents endless novelty and creativity.

Prime example: Life itself (except maybe for crabs!).

Main Characteristics of OEE:

  • Increasing diversity
  • Progressive complexification (simpler entities may persist)
  • Major breakthroughs and transitions (e.g., replicating molecules, multicellularity, language)
