Simplest Components, Invisible Fitness

Quick Introduction

Genotype is not encoded but formed by simulation rules. Components achieve predetermined reactions.

  • Emergent Genotype
  • Rule-Based Formation
  • Predetermined Reactions
  • Self-Reproduction Potential
  • Unanticipated Evolution

Example: Emergence Particle Simulation

This is an example where very simple rules can lead to something already evolving.

Example: Tom Mohr's Particle Life

No trivial intelligence layer. Long Experiment: Watch here.

Example: Conway's Game of Life (GOL)

Turing complete; anything should be possible. Evolution is harder to achieve predictably.

Example: Lenia & Flow Lenia

Flow Lenia: mass conservative lenia which can be used for evolving creatures.

Link with Open-Ended Evolution

Often hard to get evolution started due to the randomness required for right reactions.

However, the fact that *anything* could be possible suggests evolution could happen in forms we can't anticipate – very exciting for OEE!

  • High potential for novelty.
  • Challenges in bootstrapping evolution.
  • Fitness is an emergent property of the system dynamics.

Advancements in Observation & Future Outlook

Sakana.ai: Employs foundation models to find simulation parameters for desired behaviors. Shows potential for biology applications.

CSFT (Clément's Lab): Uses RLHF to train models that identify 'good' simulations based on human rankings. Initial results are very promising !
